Deploying Validation Services in OpenShift AI

Automated validation services ensure ongoing model quality and catch issues before they impact customers.

You’ve validated your model manually. Now it’s time to deploy automated validation services that continuously monitor model quality and provide early warning of issues.

Why Deploy Validation Services

Manual validation is valuable, but automated validation services provide:

  • Continuous Monitoring: Validate models on every update

  • Early Warning: Catch issues before they impact customers

  • Consistency: Standardized validation across all models

  • Efficiency: Automated validation reduces manual effort

What You’ll Deploy

This chapter teaches you to deploy:

  • Validation Service: A Kubernetes service that runs validation tests

  • Validation Pipeline: Automated validation triggered by model updates

  • Validation Dashboard: Visualization of validation results

Prerequisites

Before starting, ensure:

  • You have completed Chapter 2 (validation tests run successfully)

  • Your OpenShift AI cluster is accessible

  • You have permissions to create Kubernetes resources

  • S3 storage is available for validation results

Chapter Structure

This chapter is organized into three modules:

  1. Validation Service Design: Understand the architecture

  2. Deploy Validation Service: Deploy the service in OpenShift AI

  3. Automated Validation Pipeline: Integrate validation into your workflow

Expected Outcomes

By the end of this chapter, you will have:

  • Deployed a validation service in OpenShift AI

  • Automated validation triggered by model updates

  • A validation dashboard showing ongoing model quality

  • Integration of validation into your deployment workflow

Next Steps

  • Learn about validation service design

  • Proceed to deploy the validation service
