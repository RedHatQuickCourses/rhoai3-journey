Phase 4: Governance & Compound Systems

Early Draft & Testing Notice

The validation and governance architectures presented in this phase are currently in an early draft state and are undergoing active testing. While the structural concepts and deployment methodologies are aligned with the target state of Red Hat OpenShift AI 3.x, expect to adapt some configurations as the platform’s governance capabilities (like the Guardrails Orchestrator) reach final validation.

Stop crossing your fingers when you deploy GenAI. Start engineering trust.

In the experimental phase of AI, organizations were content with "vibes-based" evaluation. A data scientist would prompt a model, read the output, and say, "Looks good to me." But you cannot build an enterprise on "looks good."

In the Industrial AI Factory, models are not magic tricks; they are high-throughput software components that carry massive liability. GenAI introduces non-deterministic risks—hallucinations, toxic injections, and PII leakage—that traditional software testing cannot catch. This is the shift from the Black Box (blind trust in public APIs) to the Glass Box (mathematical certainty and control).

Part 1: The Architecture of Trust

To industrialize AI, you must move from "monitoring" to "intercepting." In a traditional MLOps pipeline, monitoring happens after the fact. If a GenAI model leaks PII or outputs hate speech, the damage is instantaneous.

Therefore, TrustyAI functions as the nervous system of Red Hat OpenShift AI, providing an active defense layer.

  • The Guardrails Orchestrator (The Shield): This acts as a proxy that intercepts every inference request.It pauses the request, sends the prompt to a series of detectors (Regex, Toxicity, PII), and waits for a verdict If blocked, the request is rejected immediately, and the LLM never sees it, saving GPU compute costs.

  • The Model Serving Runtime (The Engine): The model only accepts traffic from the Guardrails Orchestrator.

  • TrustyAI Service (The Auditor): This passive layer consumes payload data to calculate long-term metrics like drift and bias, writing them to Prometheus.

Part 2: The Three Pillars of Governance Value

By implementing this architecture, you unlock three critical capabilities that public APIs cannot provide:

  1. Economic Viability (The Shield): Malicious actors or broken apps can flood your models, draining your budget. Because the Guardrails Orchestrator blocks toxic prompts before they hit the GPU, you stop paying for "bad" inference.

  2. Sovereignty & Security (The Perimeter): TrustyAI runs entirely within your OpenShift cluster, ensuring total disconnection from public networks. Your data never leaves your VPC.

  3. Quantitative Risk (The Audit): Executives need numbers, not qualitative assurances. By integrating security for AI (AIMI), TrustyAI performs automated Red Teaming to mathematically calculate a risk score. You replace "I think it’s safe" with a verified statistical risk metric.

Part 3: The Taxonomy of Risk & Countermeasures

Before we configure the operator, we must define what we are measuring. "Bad AI" comes in three distinct flavors in the enterprise:

Risk Term Definition Factory Countermeasure

Hallucination

The model confidently generates false information.

LM-Eval (Factuality)

Toxicity / Jailbreak

The model is tricked into generating hate speech or phishing emails.

Bias / Drift

Part 4: The Accuracy Exam (LM-Eval)

Deployment proves the pipes work; evaluation proves the water is clean. In this phase, you will use the LMEvalJob Custom Resource to administer two types of exams: 1. The Standardized Test: Using public benchmarks like MMLU for general reasoning. 2. The Custom Exam: Creating domain-specific tests (like "OpenShift Trivia") using a task.yaml configuration.

The Engineering Outcome

By the end of this phase, you will have locked down your production engine.

  • Checkpoint: You will deploy the Guardrails Orchestrator in "Service Mode" (decoupling it from the model for easier updates), intercept a live toxic prompt, and successfully run an LMEvalJob benchmark to prove your model’s reasoning capabilities.

With governance in place, your AI Factory is now a trusted launchpad. You are ready to start building autonomous Agentic applications and Compound AI Systems securely on top of this foundation.
