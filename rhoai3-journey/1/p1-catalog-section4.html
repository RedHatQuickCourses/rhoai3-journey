Simulate the Catalog Experience

Experience the workflows that make this possible.

To truly understand the value of the OpenShift AI model catalog, you must experience how it replaces trial-and-error with mathematical, governed workflows.

Below are focused, interactive simulations. Step into the shoes of both the AI Engineer and the Platform Administrator to see how OpenShift AI secures the supply chain from discovery to deployment.

Workflow 1: Exploring the Model Card (Transparency)

The Goal: Evaluate a model’s capabilities, limitations, and reproduction steps before deciding to deploy it.

The Value: Eliminating the "black box" of AI by providing deep visibility into Red Hat Validated assets.

Simulation 1: The Model Card Walkthrough

  • Observe how the catalog separates Red Hat Validated models from community options.

  • Explore the model card to review the Model Overview, optimization details (like Quantization), and embedded deployment examples.

  • Review the benchmark data to see how the quantized model performed against the original model.

Workflow 2: Data-Driven Provisioning (Performance Insights)

The Goal: Determine the exact hardware required to meet a specific Service Level Agreement (SLA).

The Value: Preventing expensive over-provisioning and avoiding under-resourced pod crashes.

Simulation 2: Sizing the Hardware

  • Navigate to the Performance Insights tab for the Llama 3 model.

  • Adjust the Max Latency and Min RPS sliders.

  • Watch how hardware configurations (like the H200 or A100) that fail to meet your specific token generation requirements are instantly hidden, removing the guesswork from capacity planning.

Workflow 3: Enterprise Governance & Catalog Sources

The Goal: Take control of the catalog as a Platform Administrator to enforce strict corporate compliance. The Value: Securing the AI supply chain by explicitly controlling which models are visible and permitted for deployment.

Simulation 3: AI Catalog Sources

  • Click through the simulation below.

  • See how administrators can use the new 3.3 UI to inject custom YAML catalog sources, exposing internal, fine-tuned models (e.g., "Acme Finance 7B").

  • Observe the Allow/Disallow capabilities in action as the administrator explicitly disables the default Red Hat AI models to enforce strict regulatory restrictions.
