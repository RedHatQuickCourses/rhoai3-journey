Defining Business Requirements for Validation

Business requirements drive validation—not the other way around.

Before you can validate a model, you must understand what "good" means for your business application.

Identifying Business Requirements

Step 1: Define Your Use Case

Start by clearly defining your application:

  • What problem are you solving? (e.g., customer support, document analysis, code generation)

  • Who are your users? (e.g., internal employees, external customers, developers)

  • What is the expected workflow? (e.g., real-time chat, batch processing, API integration)

Step 2: Identify Success Criteria

Define what success looks like for your application:

  • Accuracy Requirements: What level of correctness is acceptable?

  • Performance Requirements: What latency and throughput are needed?

  • Quality Requirements: What safety and compliance standards must be met?

  • Cost Requirements: What is the acceptable cost per request?

Step 3: Identify Failure Modes

Understand what can go wrong:

  • Accuracy Failures: Incorrect or misleading responses

  • Performance Failures: Responses too slow for the use case

  • Quality Failures: Responses that violate safety or compliance standards

  • Cost Failures: Deployment costs exceed business value

Example: Customer Support Application

Use Case Definition

  • Problem: Automate customer support responses

  • Users: External customers seeking help

  • Workflow: Real-time chat interface

Success Criteria

  • Accuracy: >90% of responses are helpful and correct

  • Performance: <2 second response time for 95% of requests

  • Quality: No responses that violate company policy

  • Cost: <$0.01 per request

Failure Modes

  • Accuracy: Providing incorrect information to customers

  • Performance: Slow responses frustrate customers

  • Quality: Responses that damage brand reputation

  • Cost: Deployment costs exceed savings from automation

Example: Document Analysis Application

Use Case Definition

  • Problem: Extract and analyze information from legal documents

  • Users: Legal professionals

  • Workflow: Batch processing of document sets

Success Criteria

  • Accuracy: >95% accuracy on key information extraction

  • Performance: Process 100 documents per hour

  • Quality: Maintain confidentiality and compliance with legal standards

  • Cost: Reduce manual review time by 70%

Failure Modes

  • Accuracy: Missing critical information in documents

  • Performance: Processing too slow for business needs

  • Quality: Violating confidentiality or compliance requirements

  • Cost: Automation costs exceed manual processing costs

Translating Requirements to Validation Criteria

Once you have business requirements, translate them into validation criteria:

Accuracy Criteria

  • General Accuracy: Test on standard benchmarks

  • Domain Accuracy: Test on domain-specific datasets

  • Use Case Accuracy: Test on real-world scenarios

Performance Criteria

  • Latency: Measure time to first token and inter-token latency

  • Throughput: Measure requests per second and tokens per second

  • Resource Utilization: Measure GPU and memory usage

Quality Criteria

  • Safety: Test for harmful or inappropriate responses

  • Compliance: Test for regulatory requirement adherence

  • Consistency: Test for response consistency across similar inputs
