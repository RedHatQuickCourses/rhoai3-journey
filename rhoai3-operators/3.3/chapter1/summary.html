Summary: The MaaS Operator Foundation

Congratulations! You have successfully laid the groundwork required to run a high-availability OpenShift AI cluster. By transitioning from manual configurations to automated, operator-driven infrastructure, you have built a system capable of producing AI tokens at scale.

The "MaaS Factory" Architecture Recap

Building this foundation required a strict order of operations. Let’s review the stack you have assembled:

  1. The Hardware Layer: The Node Feature Discovery (NFD) operator scans your nodes and applies specific hardware labels. The GPU operator watches for these NFD labels to inject specific driver daemonsets and device plugins.

  2. The Networking & Governance Layer: You deployed the Cert-manager operator to automatically provision and rotate TLS certificates. You also installed the Red Hat Connectivity Link operator to manage Kuadrant, which intercepts incoming traffic to validate JSON web tokens (JWTs) and enforce rate limits.

  3. The Workload Coordination Layer: The LeaderWorkerSet (LWS) operator was deployed to coordinate multi-node distributed inference pods. You also integrated Kueue to act as a traffic controller that holds jobs in a queue until the required hardware is available.

  4. The Orchestrator: Finally, you deployed the Red Hat OpenShift AI Operator to manage the overarching AI lifecycle platform.

The Deployment Matrix

As you troubleshoot or expand this platform, use this matrix to recall how each operator functions and is activated via its specific Custom Resource (CR) and namespace architecture.

Goal Operator Implementation Detail

Prerequisite CLI Access

Web Terminal

Installed via the UI to provide a secure, in-cluster command-line interface directly within the browser.

Hardware Discovery

Node Feature Discovery (NFD)

Activated by the NodeFeatureDiscovery CR to spin up daemonsets across worker nodes.

Hardware Acceleration

NVIDIA GPU Operator

Activated by the ClusterPolicy CR to manage drivers based on NFD labels.

Automated TLS/Certificates

Cert-Manager Operator

Autonomous out-of-the-box. Automatically generates a CertManager CR to provision and rotate certificates.

Security & Rate Limiting

Connectivity Link (Kuadrant)

Installed in kuadrant-system and activated by an empty Kuadrant CR spec for optimal defaults.

Job Queuing & Scheduling

Red Hat build of Kueue

Provides central workload scheduling, job queues, and quota management to prevent GPU contention.

Distributed Computing

LeaderWorkerSet (LWS)

Activated by a LeaderWorkerSetOperator CR named cluster to manage distributed runtime pods.

The Orchestrator

Red Hat OpenShift AI

Installed in redhat-ods-operator. Remains dormant until activated by the DataScienceCluster (DSC) and DSCInitialization (DSCI) CRs in Phase 1.

Your Phase 0 Final Checklist

Before moving on to the data science configuration, confirm your infrastructure is healthy:

  • Discovery: NFD master and worker pods are running. Worker nodes expose accelerator labels.

  • Operator Health: All operator ClusterServiceVersions (CSVs) in their respective namespaces show a phase of Succeeded.

  • Autonomy: Cert-manager automatically generated a CertManager custom resource. It successfully spun up pods in the cert-manager namespace.

  • Control Plane Readiness: The rhods-operator pod is running in the redhat-ods-operator namespace, waiting for your commands.

Activating the Data Plane

Red Hat OpenShift AI depencies are complete, and you are ready for Phase 1 — Assembling the factory.
