The Model Catalog: Curating the AI Supply Chain

Reduce guesswork. Optimize hardware. Deploy with confidence.

In the enterprise AI landscape, the challenge is no longer just finding a model—it is ensuring that model is safe, performant, and cost-effective.

While a Model Registry acts as a centralized repository for tracking and governing the lifecycle of your organization’s specific assets (the "Title Office"), the Red Hat OpenShift AI Model Catalog serves a distinct, complementary purpose. It is a curated, secure library of pre-evaluated generative AI models from providers like Red Hat, IBM, Meta, NVIDIA, and Google (the "Showroom").

The core business value of the catalog lies in reducing the time, cost, and operational risk associated with GenAI deployments through three key pillars.

Figure 1. Model Catalog and Red Hat Validated AI Models for Generative AI

1. Validated & Enterprise-Ready

Deploying raw, community-sourced models introduces "Shadow AI" risks. The models in the catalog are not just listed; they are Red Hat Validated Models.

  • Rigorously Tested: Verified for accuracy using the Language Model Evaluation Harness and performance-benchmarked using GuideLLM.

  • Secure Supply Chain: Scanned for vulnerabilities and packaged in standardized OCI formats (ModelCars) to integrate seamlessly into managed software lifecycles.

  • Lifecycle Management: Integrated into a supported ecosystem for long-term updates, bypassing the complex integration hurdles of deploying raw, untested open-source models.

2. Optimization & Quantization (TCO)

Hardware availability is the primary constraint for GenAI. The catalog addresses this by offering models optimized via Red Hat’s LLM Compressor toolkit.

  • Lower VRAM Requirements: By applying advanced quantization techniques to compress model weights into lower-precision formats like INT4, INT8, and FP8 Dynamic, the catalog allows massive models to run on fewer or less expensive hardware accelerators.

  • High Throughput: These optimizations maintain accuracy while significantly increasing inference speed, directly lowering your Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) per token.

3. Transparency via Model Cards

To eliminate the "black box" of AI, every asset in the catalog is paired with a detailed Model Card.

  • Performance Insights: Access benchmark data that allows you to compare metrics across specific hardware configurations before deployment.

  • Capacity Planning: Use this data to accurately size your cluster, enabling highly accurate capacity planning and hardware selection.

  • Clear Limitations: Understand the model’s architecture, intended use, and training parameters to ensure policy compliance.

Your Mission: Operationalize the Catalog

In this module, you will move beyond simply browsing models. You will learn to use the catalog as a tool for architectural decision-making and supply chain management.

You will execute the following workflow:

  1. Test Drive (Simulation): Use an interactive Arcade simulation to navigate Model Cards, interpret performance benchmarks, and size hardware requirements.

  2. Extend the Supply Chain (Lab): Take on the role of a Platform Engineer to inject a Custom Private Source into the catalog, making your organization’s fine-tuned models discoverable alongside Red Hat’s validated content.
